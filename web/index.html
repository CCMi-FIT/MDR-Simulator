When selecting a donor, numerous human leukocyte antigens (HLA) and numerous non-HLA factors are considered, although the most important is a HLA match between a donor and a patient (stem cells recipient). The HLA complex is known to be the most polymorphic genetic system in humans. It is located within the 6p21.3 region on the short arm of human chromosome 6 and contains more than 220 genes of diverse function (the variant form of a given gene is an allele).
